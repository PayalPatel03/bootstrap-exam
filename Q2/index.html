Blue Bird

Birds are of the class Aves, meaning birds are warm-blooded vertebrates. Order: Aves is segregated into 23 bird orders.

Deer

Deer are specialized herbivores, as is reflected in their large and anatomically complex digestive organs, their mobile lips.

Butterfly

Butterflies, along with the moths and the skippers, make up the insect order Lepidoptera. Butterflies are nearly worldwide in their distribution.

Cactus

One of the most unique things about cacti is their ability to store large amounts of water in their stems. This adaptation helps them to survive in desert conditions where water is scarce.

Blue Waves

Blue flashes occur when waves or predators move around dinoflagellates. These movements lead to a chemical reaction that produces light.

Sunflowers

It is a very fast growing plant; in fact, it requires around only 100 days from planting to reach maturity. Flower heads from young sunflower plants actually follow the movement of the sun during the day.
